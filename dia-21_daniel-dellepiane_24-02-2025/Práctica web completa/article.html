PANTIN CLASSIC

The most famous surf competition in Spain

Pantín Classic Galicia Pro is an internationally renowned surfing event held annually at Pantín Beach in Galicia, Spain. Since its inception in 1988, this competition has attracted top surfers from around the world, establishing itself as a key stop on the European circuit of the World Surf League (WSL). In 2024, the Pantín Classic celebrated its 37th edition from August 24 to September 1. Over 97,000 visitors attended the event, a staggering number compared to Pantín’s small population of approximately 587 residents. During the competition, the town saw a population increase of 165 times, transforming it into a lively hub of activity.

Economic and Social Impact

The economic impact of the Pantín Classic on the local community is significant. The 2024 edition created around 100 direct jobs and 500 indirect jobs, involving local businesses in transportation, logistics, audiovisual services, and catering. Additionally, nearly 200 professional surfers participated, along with their families and teams, leading to an almost 90% hotel occupancy rate in the surrounding areas. Beyond the competition, the event offers a variety of entertainment activities, including skateboarding areas, introductory surf lessons, live concerts, and dining experiences. This blend of sports and leisure makes the Pantín Classic a complete experience for surf fans and families alike.

Commitment to Sustainability

A distinguishing feature of the Pantín Classic is its strong commitment to environmental sustainability. In 2024, the event partnered with Vazva, a sustainable clothing brand, to create an exclusive collection inspired by Pantín’s natural landscape. Designed by local artist Almu Portal, the collection was made using eco-friendly materials and locally produced, reinforcing the tournament’s environmental awareness.

A Rich Surfing Legacy

In 2022, the Pantín Classic celebrated its 35th anniversary, highlighting its long history and dedication to its origins. To mark the occasion, a special edition book was published, featuring unseen images, anecdotes, and contributions from legendary surfers like Tom Curren, Sunny Garcia, and John John Florence.

Conclusion

The Pantín Classic Galicia Pro is more than just a high-level surfing competition; it is a cultural and social event that enriches the local community, promotes environmental awareness, and celebrates Galicia’s deep surfing tradition.
